# SF Spinal MRI Findings Mimicking Multiple Myeloma — Case Report

## Summary

Case report of a 50-year-old Indian female with progressive spinal complications. MRI showed diffuse heterogeneous marrow signal in lumbosacral vertebrae — initially misdiagnosed as **multiple myeloma** (neoplastic bone marrow infiltration). Further investigation revealed the patient and family members had systemic skeletal fluorosis. Multiple myeloma ruled out by negative serum protein electrophoresis.

### Key Clinical Findings
- MRI: coarse trabeculation, hypointense on T1W, mixed intensity on T2W
- Vertebral body height reduction (L2-L5), mid-body wedging, biconcave disk margins
- Spinal cord compression by osteophytes, thickened posterior longitudinal ligament, thickened ligamentum flavum
- Compressive myeloradiculopathy as primary complication

## Relevance

- Pure clinical case report — not a DL/Method paper, low method relevance
- Useful for **Introduction/Discussion**: illustrates SF can mimic malignancy on MRI, reinforcing the need for automated, objective diagnostic tools
- Documents SF spinal MRI appearance — complements X-ray-based diagnosis in our SF dataset

## Key Takeaway

SF can present with pseudo-multiple myeloma MRI findings. This diagnostic confusion risk further motivates automated SF grading systems to reduce misdiagnosis.
