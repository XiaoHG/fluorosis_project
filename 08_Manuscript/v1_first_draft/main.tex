\begin{abstract}
Automated diagnosis of endemic fluorosis---both dental fluorosis (DF) and skeletal fluorosis (SF)---poses a challenging ordinal classification problem complicated by inter-rater variability and small datasets. Existing deep learning approaches rely on standard cross-entropy classification, producing point estimates without uncertainty quantification and ignoring the ordinal nature of fluorosis grading (Normal $<$ Mild $<$ Moderate $<$ Severe). We propose a unified framework combining Evidential Deep Learning (EDL) with Ordinal Regression for Calibration and Unimodality (ORCU) via a shared-logit architecture. A single evidence head outputs Dirichlet concentration parameters $\alpha$ that serve dual purposes: the EDL path derives prediction, evidence, and uncertainty from $\alpha$, while the ORCU path applies soft ordinal encoding and log-barrier regularization on the shared logits $z$. We further incorporate multi-rater soft labels from five radiologists to capture annotation uncertainty. On a 200-image DF dataset and an 80-image SF dataset with 5-radiologist annotations, our method achieves superior calibrated accuracy, provides informative uncertainty estimates, and enforces unimodal predictive distributions through ordinal constraints. The proposed framework is backbone-agnostic and demonstrates that uncertainty-aware ordinal diagnosis is feasible even with fewer than 100 training samples.

\textbf{Keywords:} Evidential Deep Learning, Ordinal Regression, Uncertainty Quantification, Dental Fluorosis, Skeletal Fluorosis, Multi-Rater Annotation
\end{abstract}
